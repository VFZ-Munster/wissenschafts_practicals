Task 4

Calculate the risk difference, relative risk, and odds ratio for the association between diabetes and periodontitis.

üí° In SPSS:

ANALYSIEREN ‚Üí DESKRIPTIVE STATISTIKEN ‚Üí KREUZTABELLEN

Then in the Crosstabs dialog, click [Cells‚Ä¶] and under Percentages, select Row, then click Continue.
Next, click [Statistics‚Ä¶], check Risk, and click Continue.

Calculate manually:

Risk difference (RD)

Subtract the percentage values: \[ RD = p_{\text{diabetic w/ perio}} - p_{\text{non-diabetic w/ perio}} \]

Relative risk (RR)

Divide the percentage values: \[ RR = \frac{p_{\text{diabetic w/ perio}}}{p_{\text{non-diabetic w/ perio}}} \]

Odds ratio (OR)
The ratio of odds between the two groups:

The odds of periodontitis for diabetics: \[ \text{Odds}_{\text{diabetic}} = \frac{\text{No. diabetic w/ periodontitis}} {\text{No. diabetic w/o periodontitis}} \]

Divided by the odds of periodontitis for non-diabetics: \[ \text{Odds}_{\text{non-diabetic}} = \frac{\text{No. non-diabetic w/ periodontitis}} {\text{No. non-diabetic w/o periodontitis}} \]

Final odds ratio \[ OR=\frac{\text{Odds}_{\text{diabetic}}}{\text{Odds}_{\text{non-diabetic}}} = \frac{a/b}{c/d} = \frac{a \times d}{b \times c} \]

Manual calculations

\[ RD = 25.6\% - 13.6\% = 12\% \]


- ‚ö†Ô∏è Remember, this may only be an apparent association - The risk of periodontitis is 12 percentage points higher in the exposed group than in the unexposed group. - This means for every 100 people, about 12 more cases of periodontitis occur among those with diabetes.

\[ RR = \frac{25.6\%}{13.6\%} = 1.88 \]


- Individuals with diabetes have 1.88 times the risk of periodontitis compared with those without diabetes.

\[ OR = \frac{25.6/74.4}{13.6/86.4} = \frac{25.6 \times 86.4}{74.4 \times 13.6} = \frac{2211.84}{1011.84} = 2.19 \]


- People with diabetes have 2.19 times higher odds of having periodontitis compared with those without diabetes.
